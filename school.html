Education

Sunday Islamic School

Nurturing confident, compassionate American Muslims through faith-centered education — grades K through 10.

Our Mission

Rooted in Faith, Ready for the World

Our Sunday school aims to develop students who are grounded in Islamic values, intellectually curious, and engaged with their communities. We create an inclusive, inquiry-friendly environment that celebrates diverse perspectives while building a strong foundation in faith.

Subject 1

Islamic Arabic

Students build familiarity with the Arabic language and develop a deep appreciation for the Quran through memorization and translation. Advanced students explore tafsir — the interpretation and exegesis of Quranic verses — to understand the deeper meanings of the text.

Subject 2

Religion

Designed to cultivate both emotional and intellectual connections to Islam, Religion classes use group activities and student-centered learning to explore Islamic history, values, and practice. Students also build meaningful social bonds with peers in a supportive environment.

Schedule & Access

School Details

Schedule

Classes run Sundays, September through early June. Each student attends two weekly classes: Islamic Arabic and Religion. Zuhr prayer is held at 12:30 PM for all students and families.

Who Can Enroll

Sunday school is open to students in grades K through 10. Families do not need to be ICB members to enroll, though membership is encouraged and provides additional benefits.

Documents

Parent guidelines and detailed curriculum documents for both Islamic Arabic and Religion classes are available upon request or through the SunWebApp parent portal after enrollment.
