Frequently Asked Questions

What should I wear when visiting the mosque?
Modest dress is appreciated for all visitors. Women are asked to cover their hair (a scarf can be provided if needed). Both men and women should avoid sleeveless tops and shorts. Shoes are removed before entering the prayer hall.
Can non-Muslims visit the mosque?
Absolutely. ICB Wayland warmly welcomes non-Muslim visitors. We offer structured visits for school groups, interfaith groups, and individuals curious about Islam. Please use our outreach forms to schedule a visit.
What are the Five Pillars of Islam?
The Five Pillars are: Shahada (declaration of faith), Salah (prayer five times daily), Zakat (charitable giving), Sawm (fasting during Ramadan), and Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime for those able).
How do Muslims pray?
Muslims pray five times daily facing Mecca. Prayer involves standing, bowing, and prostrating while reciting verses from the Quran in Arabic. Ritual purification (wudu) is performed before prayer. Congregational prayer is performed together at the mosque.
What is Ramadan?
Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, during which Muslims fast from dawn to sunset. It is a time of spiritual reflection, increased devotion, and community. The fast is broken each evening with a meal called Iftar. Ramadan ends with Eid al-Fitr, a day of celebration.
How can I ask questions about Islam?
We welcome all questions. You can submit questions through our outreach form, attend one of our open visitor sessions, or contact us directly by email. No question is too basic — we are here to help.
