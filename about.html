Our Story

About ICB Wayland

A community rooted in faith, service, and belonging since 1982.

Who We Are

More Than a Mosque

The Islamic Center of Boston in Wayland has been a home for Muslims in eastern Massachusetts since 1982. We gather here for prayer, raise our children here, and open our doors to anyone who wants to learn about Islam or simply visit.

Over the years ICB has grown into a full community center — with daily prayers, a Sunday school for children in grades K–10, youth programs, and regular outreach with local schools and neighbors of all faiths. Everyone is welcome.

Our Commitments

What We Stand For

Faith & Worship

We provide a welcoming space for daily congregational prayers, Friday Jumu'ah, Ramadan programs, Eid celebrations, and other Islamic observances throughout the year.

Community Building

From youth programs to community dinners, we create spaces where families connect, friendships deepen, and everyone feels they belong.

Interfaith Outreach

We actively engage with neighbors, schools, and institutions across eastern Massachusetts through visits, speaker programs, and collaborative community initiatives.

Islamic Education

Our Sunday school serves students in grades K–10 with a curriculum designed to develop knowledgeable, confident, and compassionate American Muslims.

Service & Charity

We believe in giving back. Through fundraisers, humanitarian walks, and local partnerships, we work to make a positive difference beyond our walls.

Youth Development

We invest in the next generation through youth group activities, college scholarships, and mentorship programs that prepare young Muslims to lead with confidence.

In the Community

A Recognized Community Presence

ICB Wayland has been featured in local and regional media for its commitment to interfaith dialogue and community service.

Media Coverage

Featured in numerous news articles from 2015–2026 highlighting ICB's positive relationships with local organizations, schools, and town leadership.

Muhsen Partnership

ICB Wayland proudly partners with Muhsen, an organization dedicated to including individuals with disabilities in the Muslim community.

Interfaith Relations

Long-standing relationships with synagogues, churches, and civic organizations throughout eastern Massachusetts. We believe in neighborly dialogue.
